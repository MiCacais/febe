## 🎯 The Core Problem

Modern tech recruiting is broken. Companies regularly subject candidates to grueling 5-to-8-round gauntlets, complete with abstract algorithmic trivia, high-pressure live coding performances, and rigid behavioral traps. 

The result? **Leadership sits in a room three months later, genuinely baffled as to why their "perfect score" new hire is struggling to ship a single stable feature to production.**

By treating the hiring pipeline like a high-stakes theatrical showcase, the industry has optimized its filters to select for **interview performers, not real-world executioners.** This broken system actively penalizes the most valuable builders, like:
* **The Quiet Executioners:** Deeply analytical, pragmatic engineers who might be shy, introverted, or neurodivergent. They excel in a state of quiet focus but are filtered out by artificial, high-pressure social showmanship.
* **The Proven Professionals:** Senior practitioners who give 100% to their current delivery goals or handle personal and caregiving boundaries. They do not have 20+ hours a week of unpaid free time to memorize standardized puzzle patterns just to prove they can do the job they have successfully performed for a decade.

**FeBe (For Engineers By Engineers)** is an open-source, lightweight alternative designed to eliminate corporate ambiguity and redundancy, slash hiring costs, and restore respect, sanity, and inclusivity to the engineering profession.

---

## 🔱 The 3 Golden Rules of FeBe

More rounds do not yield better predictive data; they only introduce redundancy, decision paralysis, and high candidate drop-off rates. Every FeBe-compliant pipeline must strictly adhere to three non-negotiable principles:

### 1. Maximum of 3 Rounds Total
The entire pipeline consists of exactly three sequential checkpoints. No surprise panel interviews, no additional leadership syncs, and no "extra steps" because a committee is indecisive.
1. **The Alignment Conversation** (Recruiter or Engineering Lead)
2. **The Execution Evaluation** (The ONLY Technical Assessment)
3. **The Strategic Alignment Sync** (Hiring Manager / Director)

### 2. Choose Exactly ONE Technical Evaluation Track
To eliminate the technical debt of hiring, companies must identify the *single most critical technical skill* required for the role on Day 1. You cannot mix tracks. You choose **ONE** of the following options, provide clear preparation expectations to the candidate, and evaluate nothing else:
* Background Architecture Discussion
* Core Domain Trivia / Code Review Collaboration
* Practical System Design
* Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) Practice
* Foundational Problem Solving - Algorithms/Data Structures (with explicit focus areas shared in advance)
* Fully Compensated Take-Home Assessment

### 3. Ban the "Behavioral Screening Traps" in Round 1
The first round is not an interrogation or a subjective checklist for corporate masking. It is a structured, two-way alignment conversation to evaluate mutual goals, day-to-day delivery expectations, and transparency regarding compensation and culture.

## 📊 The FeBe Selection Matrix

To maximize delivery alignment, the chosen technical evaluation track must match the operational scale of the company and the seniority level of the target profile. 

| Company Stage | Junior / Fresh Grad Level | Mid-Level / Core Engineer | Senior / Staff Architect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Startup / Seed to Series A** | Foundational Logic *or* Mini Paid Take-Home | Core Domain Trivia / Code Review Collaboration | Practical System Design *or* Paid Take-Home |
| **Growth Scale-Up / Series B+** | Foundational Problem Solving (With prep guides) | Object-Oriented Programming Practice | Practical System Design |
| **Enterprise / Mature Corporate** | Foundational Problem Solving (Standardized) | Core Domain Trivia *or* OOP Practice | Background Architecture Discussion |

### 🛠️ Strategic Alignment Guardrails:
* **The Startup Rule:** Early-stage startups are strictly banned from using abstract algorithmic riddles. Startups do not need engineers to optimize for imaginary scale; they need pragmatic, autonomous builders who can navigate ambiguous codebases and ship reliable features fast.
* **The Senior Rule:** Senior and Staff candidates should be evaluated on trade-offs, architecture, and maintenance realities via a System Design or past project deep dive. Algorithmic trivia tells you nothing about their leadership execution.
* **The Fresh Grad Exception:** Standard problem-solving logic is highly acceptable for junior talent lacking production experience, provided clear blueprints of what to prepare are shared transparently upfront.

## ⚙️ Execution Constraints & Guardrails

To prevent corporate pipelines from slipping back into high-friction habits, FeBe enforces strict operational constraints:

* **Compensated Take-Homes:** If a company selects the Take-Home Assessment track, the scope **MUST** be capped at a maximum of 2 to 3 hours of real-world implementation work. Furthermore, the candidate **MUST** be financially compensated for their time at a fair market consulting rate. Unpaid homework assignments are explicitly non-compliant.
* **Pragmatic Grading:** Technical interviewers must use objective rubrics that grade on trade-off communication, readability, and structural execution. Subjective grading criteria based on verbal slickness, presentation charisma, or "culture fit" are rejected.
