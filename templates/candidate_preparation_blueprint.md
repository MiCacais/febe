We believe that great software engineering is an open-book, collaborative discipline. In production, you are never locked in a room without documentation.

Below is the exact blueprint of what we will evaluate, what technologies we will use, and exactly what you should (and shouldn't) spend your time preparing.

### 1. The Chosen Technical Track
For this role, our team evaluates candidates strictly using the **[Insert Track: e.g., Practical System Design / Core Domain Code Review]** track. We will not surprise you with any other styles of questioning.

### 2. Exact Scope of the Interview

### 3. What You SHOULD Prepare / Refresh
To do your best in this interview, we recommend refreshing your knowledge on:
* How **[Database/Tech chosen, e.g., PostgreSQL]** handles isolation levels and locking under heavy read/write loads.
* Our core tech stack for this role is **[Insert Stack, e.g., React, TypeScript, and Node.js]**. You should be comfortable discussing standard architectural patterns within this ecosystem.

### 4. What You Should NOT Waste Time Studying
Do **not** spend your time grinding the following:
* ❌ Abstract algorithmic puzzles or competitive programming riddles (LeetCode/HackerRank style).
* ❌ Complex, hyper-scale hypotheticals (We will not ask you to "design a global video streaming platform from scratch").
* ❌ Syntax memorization. You are welcome to look up documentation or use Google during the session just like you would on the job.

### 5. How You Will Be Evaluated
Our engineering team uses a strict, objective rubric. We are looking for **Quiet Executioners** who focus on:
* **Pragmatism:** Can you balance "perfect" architecture against real-world product deadlines?
* **Trade-off Analysis:** Can you clearly articulate *why* you chose one approach over another, including the downsides of your choice?
* **Collaboration:** Do you treat our interviewers as peer engineering partners rather than panel judges?

We look forward to building something together. Let us know if you have any questions before the session!
