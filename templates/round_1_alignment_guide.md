# 🤝 FeBe Round 1: Mutual Alignment Conversation Guide

**Purpose:** This document provides a structured framework for recruiters, talent partners, or engineering managers running the initial 30-minute introductory call. 

Under the FeBe methodology, **this round is strictly a non-technical, two-way alignment conversation.** It is not a behavioral interrogation designed to trap candidates with corporate phrasing rubrics. The goal is simple: confirm that both parties share the same fundamental expectations before investing time in a technical assessment.

---

## 🚫 The Non-Negotiable Rules of Round 1

1. **No Behavioral Traps:** Do not ask leading, abstract questions like *"Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a stakeholder and how you resolved it."* These questions test for rehearsed storytelling and corporate masking, actively punishing introverted or neurodivergent talent.
2. **Transparent Compensation:** Salary ranges and core benefits must be shared transparently by the interviewer within the first 15 minutes of the call. Hidden compensation ranges are explicitly non-compliant.
3. **Clear Technical Roadmap:** The interviewer must explicitly detail which **ONE** technical track the company has chosen for Round 2, exactly what will be expected, and what the candidate should focus on to prepare.

---

## ⏰ Suggested 30-Minute Time Breakdown

* **00:00 - 05:00 | Intro & The Company’s Pitch:** Briefly introduce the team, the immediate problem the team is trying to solve, and the day-to-day realities of the role.
* **05:00 - 15:00 | Mutual Operational Alignment:** Discuss the candidate's core background, environmental preferences (e.g., startup autonomy vs. enterprise structure), and baseline logistical match.
* **15:00 - 20:00 | Transparency Check (Comp & Remote Status):** Explicitly align on compensation, geographic boundaries, and work arrangements to eliminate future friction.
* **20:00 - 30:00 | Technical Track Briefing & Candidate Q&A:** Explain the next step in clear detail and hand the floor entirely to the candidate.

---

## 💬 Alignment Discussion Framework

Instead of rigid behavioral questions, use these execution-focused talking points to evaluate mutual fit:

### 1. Environmental & Workflow Fit
* **Instead of:** *"How do you handle ambiguity?"*
* **Ask this:** *"Right now, our engineering team operates in an environment where [e.g., documentation is sparse and we ship multiple times a day / requirements are strictly defined by a product team]. Based on your recent engineering experience, what type of day-to-day workflow allows you to do your absolute best work?"*

### 2. Pragmatic Problem Solving
* **Instead of:** *"Tell me about a time you failed."*
* **Ask this:** *"When building software, production realities often force us to make pragmatic compromises to ship on time. Can you talk about a time in your recent career where you had to balance perfect engineering architecture against a tight business deadline?"*

---

## 📊 The Transparency Handout (Mandatory)

Before the call ends, the interviewer must provide clear answers to the following details:

* **Compensation Range:** State the exact base salary range and equity structure (if applicable).
* **The Selected Tech Track:** *"For our next round, our team uses the [System Design / Code Review / OOP] track. It will be a 60-minute session focused on [Topic]. You will be evaluated strictly on [e.g., trade-off analysis, readability]. You do not need to memorize puzzle logic or study algorithms for this."*
* **The Final Decision Timeline:** Let the candidate know exactly when they will hear back regarding a pass/fail decision (FeBe recommendation: within 48 business hours).

---

## 📝 Round 1 Scoring Sheet (Pass / Fail Only)

FeBe completely eliminates arbitrary numerical scores (e.g., "3 out of 5 on communication"). Evaluation is binary and focused strictly on operational alignment.

| Alignment Metric | Green Flags (Pass) | Red Flags (Fail) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Operational Match** | Candidate's preferred style of building and shipping matches the team's current operational state. | Candidate requires a highly structured enterprise framework, but the company is a fast-paced seed startup (or vice versa). |
| **Compensation & Logistics** | Expectations fall comfortably within the approved team budget and scheduling boundaries. | Expectations heavily exceed the upper budget cap, or target work hours do not line up. |
| **Growth & Motivation** | The core challenges of this engineering role line up with what the candidate actively enjoys solving. | The candidate is explicitly looking to move away from the specific core responsibilities this role requires daily. |

**Final Outcome:** If all three categories hit "Green Flags," the candidate automatically advances to the single Execution Evaluation round. No committee debates required.
